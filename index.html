Personal Statement

I have something to confess, I don’t believe in passion. I know, it’s a peculiar thing to admit. But it is this very belief that has not only guided me to where I am today, and the impetus for driving me forward. Allow me to explain.

Let me tell you about my friend, for anonymity’s sake let’s call them Jerry. Jerry is a PhD student studying plants. Jerry loves plants. Jerry loves studying them, observing them, eating them, smoking them, and so on and so forth. What Jerry feels for plants, I must divulge, I have never felt before in my life about any one subject. It’s because of this that I finished my Undergraduate degree with a Bachelor’s in Environmental Chemistry, even though the only environmental chemistry I will do and have done since graduating is drink Pina Coladas on a beach.

When I said, I don’t believe in passion, I meant I don’t believe in it as a guide for living and structuring my life. I didn’t intend to imply that it doesn’t exist. To the Jerrys out there in the world, I salute you. As for me, following my passion in this narrow manner has only led to disappointment and listlessness. And it was in this listless state that I encountered technology in a way that allowed me to see its potential for doing good in the world. After finishing my Bachelors I found myself working at a small, but idealistic start-up that aims to galvanize civic participation in Jakarta, Indonesia; I moved to Tokyo, Japan where I witnessed and experienced how technology is being applied to help make Japan, historically an isolated place, more open to the world. All of this culminated in my decision to make a huge transition from what I studied before and pursue a MSc in Software Development at the University of Glasgow.

But my journey doesn’t end there, it’s hardly begun. I don’t believe in fate, but I am a sucker for a good story. Now, I find myself in search of an opportunity to put the lessons I’ve learned to practice building technology to help drive the creation of a socially and economically just world. If you’ve reached the end, I’m sorry for subjecting you to this exercise in vanity and self-indulgence, but thank you for your consideration and for taking time to read my honest thoughts.
